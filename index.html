About me

Abdullahi Abdulle Farah

I am A humanitarian M&E practitioner with over ten years of experience in developing strategic information systems for development and humanitarian initiatives. For major donors like USAID, ECHO, DFID/FCDO, FFO, BMZ, and other funded projects in Somalia and Sierra Leone, as well as Save the Children country offices, I oversaw the design and construction of MEAL systems. I am a certified SMART and SQUEAC survey manager, and I successfully completed more than ten SMART and SQUEAC surveys in Somalia. I contributed to the completion of 15 research projects in Somalia and the publication of 8 papers; of these, three were cluster randomized control trials.

In my role as department head, I oversee the research, evaluation, accountability, learning, and monitoring of more than fifty employees. I also lead the digitization of the system to produce evidence and learning insights based on program outcome, impact level, and field implementation.

I'm currently enrolled at AMREF International University to pursue an MPH. I graduated from the Front Field Epidemiology Training Program (FETP) cohort 6 in Somalia.

Core skills: advanced R, Stata, data visualization, Power BI, ArcGIS, and Generative AI.
